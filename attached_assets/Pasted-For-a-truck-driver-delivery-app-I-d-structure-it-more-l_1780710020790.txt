For a truck-driver delivery app, I'd structure it more like a specialized navigation platform than a standard map app.
Core Idea
Drivers and customers can drop pins for delivery locations, but each pin contains truck-specific information that helps the next driver.
Color-Coded Pin System
Use colors to instantly tell drivers what type of delivery location they're approaching:
🟢 Dry Van
Standard dock deliveries
Easy access
No special requirements
🔵 Reefer (Refrigerated)
Temperature-controlled facilities
Cold storage warehouses
Food distribution centers
🟠 Flatbed
Outdoor unloading areas
Construction sites
Manufacturing plants
🟣 LTL / Freight Terminal
Multiple-stop freight locations
Cross-dock terminals
🔴 Restricted / Difficult Access
Tight turns
Residential deliveries
Low bridges nearby
Limited parking
🟡 Hotshot / Sprinter Van
Smaller commercial deliveries
Expedited freight
⚫ Hazmat Approved
Hazmat-capable facilities
Special check-in requirements
Each Pin Should Include
When a user creates a pin:
Exact truck parking location
Photos of entrance
Photos of dock
Check-in office location
Gate codes (if allowed)
Delivery hours
Best approach route
Scale location
Overnight parking availability
Restroom availability
Notes from other drivers
Example:
ABC Manufacturing
Flatbed Delivery
Dock 12
Enter from Industrial Blvd
Avoid Main Street entrance
Security gate opens at 6 AM
Parking available for 10 trucks
Community Verification
Like a trucking version of Google Maps or Waze.
Drivers can:
Upvote accurate pins
Report outdated information
Upload updated photos
Add notes
Confirm access routes
The most verified pins become "trusted locations."
Revenue Model
You could offer:
Free Version
View pins
Basic navigation
Upload locations
Premium Version ($5-$15/month)
Truck-specific routing
Offline maps
Advanced filters
Company fleet tools
Delivery history
Favorite locations
Feature That Could Make It Stand Out
Instead of a single pin, allow multiple pin types at one location:
A warehouse might have:
Green pin = Dry Van dock
Orange pin = Flatbed yard
Blue pin = Reefer dock
Drivers select their equipment type and only see the pins relevant to them.
That solves one of the biggest frustrations in trucking: arriving at the correct address but still having no idea where the correct truck entrance, dock, or parking area actually is.
For your idea, I think the strongest version is a community-driven truck entrance and delivery location database built on top of Google Maps, with color-coded pins for trailer type and driver-submitted photos and notes. That would provide a clear benefit over standard navigation apps.
